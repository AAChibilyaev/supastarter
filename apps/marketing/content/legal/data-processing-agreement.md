# Data Processing Agreement

**Between:**

**[Customer Name]** , a company registered under the laws of [Country], with its registered office at [Customer Address] (hereinafter **"Controller"** );

**and**

**AACsearch** , a company registered under the laws of [Country], with its registered office at [AACsearch Address] (hereinafter **"Processor"** ).

Each a **"Party"** , collectively the **"Parties"** .

## 1. Definitions

1.1 **"Applicable Data Protection Law"** means (i) the General Data Protection Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2016/679) (**"GDPR"** ); (ii) any national implementing legislation of the GDPR; and (iii) any other applicable data protection or privacy laws, regulations, or binding codes of conduct.

1.2 **"Controller"** means the natural or legal person, public authority, agency, or other body which, alone or jointly with others, determines the purposes and means of the Processing of Personal Data.

1.3 **"Data Subject"** means an identified or identifiable natural person.

1.4 **"Personal Data"** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person (Data Subject).

1.5 **"Personal Data Breach"** means a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data transmitted, stored, or otherwise Processed.

1.6 **"Processing"** means any operation or set of operations performed on Personal Data, whether by automated means, including collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure, dissemination, erasure, or destruction.

1.7 **"Processor"** means a natural or legal person, public authority, agency, or other body which Processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.

1.8 **"Sub-processor"** means any Processor engaged by AACsearch to Process Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.

1.9 **"Supervisory Authority"** means an independent public authority established in an EEA Member State pursuant to Article 51 of the GDPR.

1.10 **"Standard Contractual Clauses"** or **"SCCs"** means the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses for the transfer of personal data to third countries pursuant to Regulation (EU) 2016/679 and Decision 2021/914 of 4 June 2021.

## 2. Subject Matter and Duration

2.1 This Data Processing Agreement (**"DPA"** ) sets out the rights and obligations of the Parties concerning the Processing of Personal Data by the Processor on behalf of the Controller in connection with AACsearch's search-as-a-service platform.

2.2 This DPA shall remain in full force and effect for the duration of the underlying service agreement between the Controller and the Processor (the **"Principal Agreement"** ), unless terminated earlier in accordance with Clause 13 below.

## 3. Processing Details

3.1 **Nature and Purpose of Processing:** The Processor provides a search-as-a-service platform that indexes, stores, and retrieves data submitted by the Controller for the purpose of enabling full-text search, faceted search, and related search functionalities on the Controller's application, website, or digital properties.

3.2 **Categories of Personal Data:** Depending on the nature of the Controller's use of the Service, the Personal Data processed may include, but is not limited to:
- Names
- Email addresses
- IP addresses
- User IDs and account identifiers
- Pseudonymized user profiles
- Search queries and browsing behavior data
- Any other Personal Data the Controller elects to submit for indexing

3.3 **Categories of Data Subjects:** Data Subjects may include:
- End users of the Controller's application or website
- Customers, clients, or visitors of the Controller
- Employees or contractors of the Controller
- Any other individuals whose Personal Data the Controller submits to the Service

3.4 **Duration of Processing:** Processing shall continue for the duration of the Principal Agreement. Upon termination, the Processor shall delete or return all Personal Data in accordance with Clause 12.

## 4. Controller Obligations

4.1 The Controller represents and warrants that:
(a) It has obtained all necessary consents and has a lawful basis for the Processing of Personal Data under Applicable Data Protection Law;
(b) It has provided all necessary privacy notices to Data Subjects;
(c) It has complied and will continue to comply with all Applicable Data Protection Law in its use of the Service;
(d) It is solely responsible for the accuracy, quality, and legality of the Personal Data submitted to the Processor.

4.2 The Controller shall be responsible for responding to Data Subject requests, except where the Processor is required by law to respond directly.

4.3 The Controller shall ensure that its instructions to the Processor comply with Applicable Data Protection Law and that the Processing of Personal Data pursuant to such instructions is lawful.

## 5. Processor Obligations

5.1 The Processor shall Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from the Controller, unless required to do otherwise by applicable law (in which case the Processor shall inform the Controller of that legal requirement before Processing, unless prohibited by law).

5.2 The Processor shall ensure that persons authorized to Process the Personal Data are subject to a binding obligation of confidentiality.

5.3 The Processor shall implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures as described in Clause 7.

5.4 The Processor shall inform the Controller immediately if, in its opinion, an instruction from the Controller infringes Applicable Data Protection Law.

## 6. Sub-processing

6.1 **General Authorization:** The Controller provides a general authorization for the Processor to engage Sub-processors. The Processor shall maintain an up-to-date list of Sub-processors, available at [https://aacsearch.com/sub-processors](https://aacsearch.com/sub-processors) or as set out in Schedule 1 to this DPA.

6.2 **Notification of Changes:** The Processor shall notify the Controller of any intended changes concerning the addition or replacement of Sub-processors at least [30] days in advance, giving the Controller an opportunity to object.

6.3 **Objection Right:** If the Controller objects to a new Sub-processor on reasonable grounds relating to data protection, and the objection is not resolved within [30] days, either Party may terminate the affected parts of the Service.

6.4 **Obligations:** The Processor shall impose, by way of a written contract, the same data protection obligations on Sub-processors as set out in this DPA. Where a Sub-processor fails to fulfill its data protection obligations, the Processor shall remain fully liable to the Controller for the performance of that Sub-processor's obligations.

## 7. Technical and Organizational Security Measures

7.1 The Processor shall implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including:

(a) **Access Control:** Role-based access controls, multi-factor authentication, and least-privilege principles for all systems Processing Personal Data.

(b) **Data Encryption:** Encryption of Personal Data at rest using AES-256 or equivalent, and in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher.

(c) **Network Security:** Firewalls, intrusion detection/prevention systems, and regular vulnerability scanning.

(d) **Physical Security:** Restricted access to data centers with 24/7 monitoring, biometric access controls, and video surveillance.

(e) **Business Continuity:** Regular backups, disaster recovery procedures, and redundancy across [geographic zones].

(f) **Software Security:** Secure software development lifecycle (SDLC) practices, regular penetration testing, and code reviews.

(g) **Personnel Security:** Background checks, confidentiality agreements, and regular data protection training for all personnel with access to Personal Data.

(h) **Logging and Monitoring:** Comprehensive audit logging of all access and operations involving Personal Data, with [12] month retention of logs.

7.2 The Processor shall regularly test, assess, and evaluate the effectiveness of these measures.

## 8. Personal Data Breach Notification

8.1 The Processor shall notify the Controller without undue delay (and in any event within [24] hours of becoming aware) of any Personal Data Breach affecting Personal Data processed on behalf of the Controller.

8.2 The notification shall include, at minimum:
(a) A description of the nature of the Personal Data Breach;
(b) The categories and approximate number of Data Subjects and records concerned;
(c) Contact information for the Processor's data protection officer or other contact point;
(d) A description of likely consequences and measures taken or proposed to address the breach.

8.3 The Processor shall cooperate fully with the Controller in investigating and remediating any Personal Data Breach and in meeting the Controller's notification obligations to the Supervisory Authority and affected Data Subjects under Applicable Data Protection Law.

## 9. Data Subject Rights

9.1 The Processor shall assist the Controller in fulfilling its obligations to respond to Data Subject requests under Applicable Data Protection Law, including rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction of processing, data portability, and objection.

9.2 The Processor shall:
(a) Promptly notify the Controller if it receives a request from a Data Subject;
(b) Not respond to the request without the Controller's prior authorization, except as required by law;
(c) Provide the Controller with reasonable assistance in responding to such requests.

## 10. Data Transfers

10.1 If the Processing of Personal Data involves transfers from the European Economic Area (**"EEA"** ) to a country outside the EEA that does not benefit from an adequacy decision under Article 45 of the GDPR, the Parties agree that the transfer shall be governed by the Standard Contractual Clauses (EU Commission Decision 2021/914), which are incorporated by reference as follows:

(a) Module Two (Controller to Processor) applies to the extent the Controller acts as a Controller and the Processor acts as a Processor;
(b) Module Three (Processor to Processor) applies to the extent the Processor engages Sub-processors;
(c) For the purposes of the SCCs, the Controller is the **"data exporter"** and the Processor is the **"data importer"** .

10.2 The information required under Annex I of the SCCs (List of Parties, Description of Transfer, Competent Supervisory Authority) is set out in Schedule 2 to this DPA.

10.3 The technical and organizational measures required under Annex II of the SCCs are set out in Clause 7 and Schedule 3 to this DPA.

10.4 Where the SCCs conflict with any provision of this DPA, the SCCs shall prevail.

## 11. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

11.1 This DPA shall be governed by the laws of [Governing Law Jurisdiction], without reference to its conflict of laws principles.

11.2 Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this DPA shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of [Jurisdiction].

## 12. Return and Deletion of Personal Data

12.1 Upon termination or expiry of the Principal Agreement, the Processor shall, at the Controller's option, either delete or return all Personal Data to the Controller within [90] days, unless applicable law requires continued storage.

12.2 "Deletion" means ensuring that Personal Data is rendered irrecoverable and inaccessible, with residual copies securely erased in accordance with industry standards (e.g., NIST SP 800-88).

12.3 The Processor may retain Personal Data to the extent required by applicable law, provided it continues to ensure confidentiality and no further Processing occurs other than as required by law.

## 13. Term and Termination

13.1 This DPA shall commence on the effective date of the Principal Agreement and shall continue until the later of:
(a) Termination of the Principal Agreement; or
(b) The date on which the Processor has ceased all Processing of Personal Data on behalf of the Controller in accordance with Clause 12.

13.2 Termination of this DPA for any reason shall not affect the provisions of this DPA that expressly or by implication survive termination.

## 14. Liability

14.1 Each Party's liability under this DPA shall be subject to the limitations set out in the Principal Agreement.

14.2 Nothing in this DPA shall limit or exclude either Party's liability for:
(a) Death or personal injury caused by its negligence;
(b) Fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation;
(c) Breaches of Applicable Data Protection Law;
(d) Any matter for which it is not lawful to limit or exclude liability.

14.3 The Processor shall be liable to the Controller for any damages caused by Processing that infringes this DPA or Applicable Data Protection Law, provided the Processor has not followed the Controller's instructions or has acted contrary to them.

## 15. Audit Rights

15.1 Upon [30] days' written notice, the Controller (or its mandated auditor) may audit the Processor's compliance with this DPA, provided that:
(a) The audit is conducted during normal business hours;
(b) The audit does not unreasonably interfere with the Processor's operations;
(c) The audit is limited to once per calendar year, except where a Personal Data Breach or regulatory investigation necessitates additional audits.

15.2 The Processor shall contribute to the costs of any audit at its own expense for up to [one] audit per calendar year.

## 16. General Provisions

16.1 This DPA constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties concerning the Processing of Personal Data and supersedes all prior agreements, understandings, and representations.

16.2 If any provision of this DPA is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

16.3 This DPA may not be amended except by a written instrument signed by both Parties.
