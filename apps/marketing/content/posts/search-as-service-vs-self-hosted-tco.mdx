The "build vs buy" decision for search infrastructure is rarely as simple as comparing monthly subscription prices. Self-hosting has hidden costs in engineering time, operations, and opportunity cost. Managed services have premium pricing but include maintenance and reliability.

This post breaks down the real TCO for both approaches across three typical deployment sizes.

## Cost Categories

A fair comparison must include:

1. **Infrastructure** — Servers, storage, bandwidth, backups
2. **Operations** — Monitoring, alerting, incident response, upgrades
3. **Engineering time** — Initial setup, tuning, maintenance, debugging
4. **Opportunity cost** — What your team could be building instead
5. **Scaling cost** — Cost to add capacity as you grow

## Scenario 1: Small Deployment (10K documents, 50K searches/mo)

### Self-Hosted (Typesense on a $10 VPS)

| Item                     | Monthly cost | Notes                                    |
| ------------------------ | ------------ | ---------------------------------------- |
| VPS (2GB RAM, 2 vCPU)    | $10          | Digital Ocean / Hetzner                  |
| Backups                  | $2           | Automated snapshot storage               |
| Monitoring               | $0           | Basic health check (free)                |
| Domain + SSL             | $1           | Certificate renewal (amortized)          |
| **Infrastructure total** | **$13/mo**   |                                          |
| Initial setup            | $200         | 4 hours of senior engineer time @ $50/hr |
| Monthly maintenance      | $50          | 1 hour/month for updates, tuning         |
| Incident response        | $25          | 30 min/month average (outages, issues)   |
| **Engineering total**    | **$275/mo**  | Amortized over 12 months                 |
| **True TCO**             | **$288/mo**  |                                          |

### Managed (AACsearch Free Tier)

| Item         | Monthly cost          |
| ------------ | --------------------- |
| Subscription | $0                    |
| Setup time   | $0 (30 min, no cost)  |
| Maintenance  | $0 (provider handled) |
| **True TCO** | **$0/mo**             |

**Verdict:** At small scale, managed is free and self-hosted costs $288/mo. No contest.

## Hidden Costs of Self-Hosting

Beyond the numbers, self-hosting carries risks that are hard to quantify until they happen:

| Risk                   | Probability | Impact                  | Mitigation cost        |
| ---------------------- | ----------- | ----------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Cluster failure        | Low         | Complete search outage  | HA setup (2x infra $)  |
| Data corruption        | Very low    | Rebuild from backup     | RTO: 2-4 hours         |
| Version upgrade broken | Medium      | Rollback or fix         | Staging env ($50/mo)   |
| Security vulnerability | Low         | Data exposure           | Patching SLA           |
| Scaling emergency      | Medium      | Performance degradation | Emergency provisioning |

Most teams underestimate these risks when making the build vs buy decision.

## Break-Even Analysis

At what scale does self-hosting become cheaper than managed?

```
Self-host fixed costs:  Setup ($200-3K) + Monthly ops ($50-500)
Managed fixed costs:    Monthly subscription ($0-249)

Break-even point: When monthly ops cost < subscription premium
```

For most teams, the break-even is never reached because:

1. Engineering time has a growth cost — every hour spent on search ops could improve your product
2. Self-hosted infrastructure costs grow with data (more servers) while managed costs stay flat
3. Incident risk increases with scale, not decreases

## The Non-Financial Factors

Sometimes self-hosting makes sense even at higher cost:

- **Compliance:** Data must never leave your infrastructure
- **Full control:** You need custom search behavior the provider doesn't support
- **Existing investment:** You already have an Elasticsearch team and cluster
- **Offline/air-gapped:** Your application runs in an isolated network

For everyone else, managed search delivers better ROI.

## Decision Framework

```text
Is search your core differentiator?
├── Yes → Self-host (you need full control and will invest)
└── No → Use managed service
        ├── < 50K docs → Free tier (AACsearch / Algolia)

```
